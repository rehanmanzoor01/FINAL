FAQs

Common Questions

When should I bring apples for storage? +
Apples should be brought for storage as soon as possible after harvest — ideally within 24–48 hours. Pre-cooling begins immediately on arrival. Our season typically runs from August through October for intake.
What apple varieties do you store? +
We store all major Kashmir apple varieties including Red Delicious, Golden Delicious, American Delicious, Fuji, Gala, and Chamur. Our CA chambers are especially suited for Delicious varieties for extended shelf life.
Is there a minimum storage quantity? +
Our minimum is 1 metric tonne (MT) for regular cold storage, and 5 MT for CA storage chambers. We accommodate both small orchard owners and large commercial growers.
How is my stock kept separate from other customers? +
Each customer's consignment is tagged, logged, and stored in a designated section. All entry and exit is recorded. CA chambers are sealed for individual or small-group consignments to maintain atmospheric integrity.
What happens during a power outage? +
We have 100% diesel backup generator coverage with automatic switchover in under 30 seconds. Temperature fluctuations during any outage are minimised and reported immediately to affected customers via SMS.
Can I visit my stock while in storage? +
Yes, facility visits can be arranged with prior notice during business hours. For CA chambers, access is limited to avoid atmospheric disruption, but we provide regular condition reports and photographs on request.
Do you offer insurance coverage for stored goods? +
We advise growers to take out their own produce insurance. We can provide documentation and storage condition records to support any insurance claims. Please speak to our team for guidance.
